# Code of conduct

BRAINS exists because mainstream technology was not built for or with neurodivergent people. This community is. The behaviour we expect, and the behaviour we will not tolerate, both follow from that.

## What we expect

- **Assume good faith.** Most friction is a difference in communication style, not malice.
- **Be direct, not blunt.** Say the thing. Then say why. Soften the start, not the substance.
- **Make space for different ways of working.** Async over synchronous. Written over verbal. Specific over vague. Process at your own pace; reply when you are ready.
- **Treat lived experience as expertise.** Neurodivergent contributors are the assumed reader, not an edge case to accommodate.
- **State your access needs.** We will meet them where we can. You do not need to justify them.
- **Credit honestly.** Cite, attribute, and link.

## What we will not tolerate

- Slurs, mockery, or jokes at the expense of neurodivergent people, people with disabilities, or any group.
- Deficit framing as the default voice — describing neurodivergence as an affliction to be cured, or support as a shortfall.
- Demanding eye contact, real-time replies, in-person meetings, or any form of communication as a test of seriousness.
- Doxxing, harassment, sustained personal hostility, or coordinated pile-ons.
- Sexualised attention or contact that is unwelcome.
- Using AI-generated images of real or fictional people in BRAINS contexts.

## How to report

Send a message to **conduct@brainscertified.com**. Include what happened, where, and any links or screenshots if you have them. You can report on someone else's behalf with their consent.

We will acknowledge within five business days. We will tell you what we plan to do, and we will tell you when it is done. If you are the person reported, you will hear from us with the substance of the complaint and you will have a chance to respond.

## Enforcement

Possible responses include:

1. A private conversation to clarify expectations.
2. A public or private warning.
3. Temporary suspension from the project.
4. Permanent removal.

We will pick the smallest response that addresses the harm.

## Scope

This applies in every BRAINS space: this repository, related repositories, community channels, events, and direct messages between contributors that arise from this work.

## Acknowledgements

This code of conduct draws on the Contributor Covenant and on the lived experience of the neurodivergent contributors who built BRAINS.
